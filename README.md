# TFSC’s Edventurefy & EI Open Source  
  
What got us here will not take us there.  
  
This is a brief overview of a much larger system developed over more than a decade.   
The founder is not technical, so parts of the architecture may be conceptually clear before they are technically precise.   
 ## Core idea  **EI - **As the world changes faster, people need clearer pathways for learning, adaptation, and contribution.  **Educational Infrastructure (EI)** seeks to be that bridge by turning today’s standards and objectives into clear skills, and those skills into objective milestones and visible pathways forward.  The result is an open-source foundation that makes progress more visible, portable, and accessible.  
  
**Edventurefy **- If school is meant to prepare people for life, learning should feel more like practice for the adventure of living. **Edventurefy** is the structure layer built around the conditions that significantly increase learning and growth: agency, relevance, challenge, ownership, and social connection. It makes skill acquisition more engaging, effective, and scalable.  
  
  
## In short  
  
**EI** is the connected infrastructure that translates:  
standards → skills → milestones → learning pathways → feedback → progress tracking → portable proof  
  
**Edventurefy** is the methodology, the structure layer that makes those pathways meaningful and learnable.  
  
  
  
## Why this is open  
  
Useful ideas spread faster than any one founder, team, or institution can build alone.  
  
This work is being released openly to help serious builders test, adapt, improve, and extend what works.  
  
  
## What this repository is  
  
This repository is the open-source home for TFSC’s decade long work on Edventurefy and EI .  
  
It is a practical skeleton for:  
- core vision and framework docs  
- working examples  
- facilitator pathways  
- builder documentation  
- open-source components  
- lightweight scripts, workflows, or tooling where relevant  
  
##   
**think of this as an ecosystem.** It meets teachers and learners wherever they are, whether surrounded by screens or sitting in a circle with paper and pencils. It turns learning into a continuous, collaborative adventure, where every challenge becomes a chance to grow, and every “Not Yet” is just another step toward mastery.
