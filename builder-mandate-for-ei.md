# Builder Mandate for EI  
  
What got us here is not going to take us there.  
  
As the world changes faster, people need clearer pathways for learning, adaptation, and contribution. EI is intended to be that bridge.  
  
This is the builder mandate.  
  
## 1. Build for real human adaptation  
  
Do not build for ideal behavior.  
Build for real behavior.  
  
Humans adapt to incentives.  
Humans adapt to constraints.  
Humans optimize for what is measured and rewarded.  
  
If the system is built around the wrong incentives, distortion will follow.  
  
## 2. Build with buffers  
  
If you scale new infrastructure without accounting for psychological adaptation and institutional lag, expect backlash.  
  
Build fast, yes.  
But build with buffers.  
  
## 3. Build for skill acquisition, not just content coverage  
  
Education should train for life, not just for tests.  
  
Learning should move from:  
- exposure  
to  
- capability  
  
## 4. Replace weak proxies with visible proof  
  
Seat time, coverage, and compliance are weak proxies.  
  
Where possible, progress should become:  
- more visible  
- more portable  
- more objective  
- more grounded in evidence  
  
## 5. Keep it usable  
  
The system should be practical.  
  
If people cannot understand it, facilitate it, adapt it, or test it, it will not spread.  
  
Favor:  
- clarity  
- modularity  
- low-friction use  
- real-world pilots  
- human-readable design  
  
## 6. Prototype in the real world  
  
The next step is not endless debate.  
  
The next step is to prototype transition infrastructure in the real world:  
- in schools  
- in communities  
- in youth programs  
- in training systems  
- in low-tech and high-tech environments  
  
## 7. Open what helps the mission spread  
  
This work is being released openly because the transition is bigger than any one founder, team, or institution.  
  
The goal is not to hoard the model.  
The goal is to spread what works, improve it in public, and make it easier for serious builders to move faster.
